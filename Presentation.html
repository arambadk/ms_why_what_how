Why?

Myth 1: Top 10 universities are SAME for all specilizations and NEED a high GRE score

Fact: This is a specilization specific question and we need granular information (Data Analysis)

Data source - image

Myth 2: Big companies hire ONLY from top universities

Fact: Hiring involves multiple factors and you should not worry about which company hires from where, just focus on being a good engineer

Data source - image

From the data source: "Want to work at Apple? Stanford’s a good bet, certainly—but so is San Jose State, which lies just a few exits east of the Apple campus off of Interstate 280. Granted, San Jose State’s enrollment is nearly twice that of Stanford, so the latter probably still gives you a better chance. But SJSU is certainly the more economical option if you’re just looking at the sheer number of alumni connections to Cupertino."

Myth 3: California/NY etc. will ALWAYS be costly relative to other states

Fact: It depends, calculate for your particular case

What?

  • Research
    • Google scholar, microsoft academic research etc.
    • Institutions associated with university eg. USC's ISI, NCSU's NRL
  • Courses
    • Breadth and depth requirements
    • Would I be able to do all the courses that I "WANT - PhD, Specific Job etc."?
  • Financial requirements
    • Tuition cost, Living expense, State min wage, Scholarships etc.
    • Pay per month options
    • PS: On campus jobs are to support your education, please understand the priority
  • Opportunities
    • Research options
    • Specilizations
    • Job (internship,co-op,full time) depth and breadth
    • Student life and culture

How?

  • Research papers published in last 5 years - google scholar, microsoft academic research
  • Research Labs/renowned faculty - university dept. web sites, no. of papers published, citations
  • Courses offered - university website
  • Tuition fees - university website (cost per credit * no. of mandatory credits), Cost of living, possible oncampus jobs (no accurate data source, assume nothing is available) - example for USC from fall 2012
  • LinkedIn Job Tool - image
  • Crime rate info - just google it :)
  • University Ranking? - refer multiple sources
  • Student life and University Culture - 'Student life' web page of the university, alumni etc.
